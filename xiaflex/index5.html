SIGNS OF DUPUYTREN’S

What does Dupuytren’s contracture look like?

Every hand is unique, but there are common signs of Dupuytren’s contracture (DC). Here are a few things you can look for if you think you might have DC:

Collagen builds up beneath the skin to form a cord.

The cord may thicken and shorten.

Fingers begin to bend toward the palm.

Which fingers can be affected?

DC can occur in one or both hands. In either hand, the condition may affect:

Any finger, but it’s most common in the ring finger and the little finger. The index finger and thumb are rarely affected

Any joint at the base of your finger and the middle of your finger
